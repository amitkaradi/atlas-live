Manifesto

Why one of one.

The web made everything copyable, so everything got copied. The same five templates wear a thousand logos. Businesses that share nothing — not a city, not a craft, not a customer — share a homepage.

We think a website can be something else. Not a rental. Not a theme with your name spray-painted on it. An object. Built once, by one pair of hands, with a point of view — and owned the way objects are owned: entirely, and alone.

So Atlas runs like a gallery, not a warehouse. The shelf is small. Every piece is already alive — real pages, real motion, a cursor that moves because someone meant it to. You are not buying a preview of work that might happen. You are buying work that already exists, and the right to be the only one who has it.

When a piece sells, its slot goes dark. The design is never resold, never re-skinned, never quietly recycled. The creator starts again, from zero, on something new — because that's the deal exclusivity makes: it costs more, and it's worth more.

One price, no fine print. Five days from claim to launch. A human doing the personalization, the same human who built it. A refund window that closes only when you launch — because confidence should be cheap to offer when the work is good.

One site. One owner. That's the entire idea. Everything else is just us keeping the lights warm.
